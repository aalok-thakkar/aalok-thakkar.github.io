Aalok Thakkar: Letters of Recommendation

I am always glad when a student I have worked with asks for a letter. A strong, specific letter takes time, though, and a few notes below are there so I can write the kind of letter you deserve.

I write Letters of Recommendation (LoRs) for students who have either:

  • taken at least three courses with me (or, at minimum, one elective course taught by me), or
  • worked directly with me on a research project (including capstone or thesis).

Please reach out at least two weeks before the earliest deadline. With shorter notice, I will not be able to write the letter I would want to write for you.

A few additional notes for graduate (or other academic) programmes:

  • I write graduate-school letters for students I have supervised on a research project. If we have not worked together in that way, the letter would be thinner than you deserve, and I would rather not write it.
  • If I have strong reservations about a particular programme, institution, or fit for you, I will tell you and gently decline to write for that specific application — the letter you want there is not one I can write honestly.

When you do reach out, please send the following at least two weeks before the first deadline:

  1. Your CV or résumé.
  2. Your unofficial transcript.
  3. Your statement of purpose. Drafts are welcome.
  4. A short note listing the courses you took with me (with semester/year and grades), any research projects we worked on (with outcomes), and anything in particular you would like me to bring forward.
  5. A shared Google spreadsheet of the institutions and programmes you are applying to, with deadlines and a column where I can mark each submission as it goes out.

Two final things, less about logistics than about how the letters work:

  • I submit letters directly to the institution or platform. I do not share the written letter, or a copy of it, with applicants. When applying to U.S. universities, please waive your right to view your letter when you send me the request — the waiver lets me write more candidly on your behalf.
  • I do not write open-ended or generic letters, including those addressed “To Whom It May Concern.” Every letter is addressed to a particular programme.

If any of this feels burdensome, please tell me. The constraints are here so each letter can be careful and specific, not to make the process harder than it needs to be.
