aalok thakkar: summer opportunities

Ashoka's extended summer break offers you a rare gift: time. Time to explore, reflect, and engage in ways that go beyond the usual rhythms of the semester. Rather than seeing this as just a pause from academics, I encourage you to think of it as an opportunityâ€”one you can shape in ways that align with your goals, curiosities, and aspirations. This page outlines some ideas:

read a book: The break provides an opportunity to engage with substantive texts beyond your coursework. Below are curated reading lists:
  1. First Summer:
    1. Feynman Lectures on Computation (Richard P. Feynman, 1996)
    2. Computing with Quantum Cats (John Gribbin, 2013)
    3. The Signal and the Noise (Nate Silver, 2012)
  2. Second Summer:
    1. Clean Code (Robert Cecil Martin, 2008)
    2. Logicomix (Apostolos Doxiadis and Christos Papadimitriou, 2008)
    3. AI Snake Oil (Arvind Narayanan and Sayash Kapoor, 2024)
  3. Third Summer:
    1. Godel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid (Douglas Hofstadter, 1979)
    2. The Design of Everyday Things (Don Norman, 1988)
    3. The Pragmatic Programmer (Andrew Hunt and David Thomas, 1999)
    4. take a course: Enrolling in Ashoka's summer term is an excellent way to fulfill credit requirements.
    1. Completing core or elective courses in summer can free up space for advanced seminars, independent study, or internships during the academic year.
    2. Smaller Class Sizes: Summer courses often have fewer students, allowing for more interaction with professors and personalized feedback.
    3. Flexibility for Research/Internship Prep: If you plan to apply for competitive programs (e.g., research internships, grad school), summer courses can help you build necessary prerequisites.
    This summer, I plan to offer Quantitative Reasoning and Mathematical Thinking (FC-0306) and Introduction to Computer Science (CS-1102).

    teach: Summer can also be used to explore teaching opportunities:
    1. The university recruits TAs for summer courses, Young Scholars' Program, Ashoka Horizons Program, Lodha Genius Program, and Ashoka X.
    2. One can also explore globally renowned mathematics programs such as Ross Mathematics Program (USA/China) and PROMYS (USA/India). These programs recruit undergrads with strong mathematical backgrounds as counselors or TAs.
    summer schools: Several summer programs offering intensive learning and research exposure:
    1. ACM India Summer Schools
    2. CSA Undergraduate Summer School Initiative at IISc. This year the summer school is focussed on Approximation Algorithms.
    research internships:
    1. You can reach out to professors at Ashoka as well as other universities to check if they have any on-going research projects that you can contribute to. A list of my projects with openings are listed here.
    2. Visiting Students Research Programme, Summer Student Research Programme, Vigyan Vidushi program, and the S. N. Bhatt Memorial Excellence Fellowship Program by TIFR and ICTS.
    3. Summer Internship Programmes at several IITs (Bhubaneswar, Dharwad, Goa, Gandhinagar, Guwahati, Kanpur, Madras, Roorkee), IIITs (Delhi, Hyderabad), IISER Kolkata, CMI, ISI, and BARC.
    4. Charpak Summer Training Scholarship
    5. IUSSTF-Viterbi Program
